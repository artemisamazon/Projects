Sea Otter

Sea Otters are the smallest marine mammals.

  • Scientific Name: Enhydra lutris
  • Weight: Adults weigh between 31-99 pounds
  • Diet: Sea urchins, mollusks, crustaceans, & fish
  • Habbit: Coasts of the Pacific ocean in North America and Asia

Sea Otters use tools such as rocks to smash their prey and eat 25% of their body weight. Sea otters can dive over 300 feet deep and can hold their breath up to 8 minutes.
